About Yerrabi District Cricket Club

Yerrabi District Cricket Club (YDCC) is a not-for-profit, volunteer-led community cricket club based in the heart of Canberra’s Gungahlin region. Established in late 2021, YDCC was founded with a clear mission: to promote cricket among children and youth under the age of 18, and to foster their holistic physical, mental, and social development through sport.

The club was proudly initiated under the leadership of the Indian Australian Multicultural Sports Association (IAMSA) — a community organisation committed to inclusive sports participation and youth development.

Affordability and Inclusion

At YDCC, we believe that cricket should be accessible to every child — regardless of their financial situation. To ensure that cost is never a barrier to participation, we offer flexible financial support options for families, particularly those from socio-economically challenged backgrounds or impacted by rising living costs. As a community-first and values-driven club, we are proud to provide inclusive pathways that empower every child to play, grow, and thrive through the sport they love.

Looking Ahead

With each season, YDCC continues to build strong pathways from grassroots to higher levels of the game. In addition to strengthening our junior programs, we are also actively preparing to expand into senior cricket — offering continued opportunities for our youth to grow into competitive senior players and for broader community engagement across all age groups.

Through improved infrastructure, professional coaching, digital learning resources, and expanded community programs, we aim to become one of Canberra’s most inclusive and future-focused cricket clubs.
